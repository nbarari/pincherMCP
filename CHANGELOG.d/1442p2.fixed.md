`neighborhood` no longer blows the MCP per-call token cap on large files — when the projected payload exceeds the ~18KB budget, the response is truncated with a clamp warning naming the budget + `next_steps` pointing at the continuation offset. Pre-fix `limit=200` on a 176-neighbor file returned a hard "exceeds maximum allowed tokens" error with no recovery path; now the caller gets a partial response + pagination hint. Composes with #1442 part 1 (`fields` projection) — lean projections let more entries fit in the same budget. At least one entry always surfaces even when a single neighbor exceeds the budget on its own (#1442 part 2).
