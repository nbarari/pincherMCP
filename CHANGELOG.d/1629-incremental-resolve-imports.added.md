Incremental resolve pass for IMPORTS edges on small-tick watcher runs (#1629 v0.87, first slice). The full incremental-resolve design is multi-day; this lands the IMPORTS resolver and the supporting store primitives (`LoadPendingEdgesByKindAndFiles`, `DeleteResolvePassEdgesByKindForSourceFiles`) that the CALLS and READS resolvers will reuse in follow-up slices. On watcher ticks where `totalFiles <= 32` and `force=false`, the resolver loads only the IMPORTS pending edges from re-extracted files and wipes only those files' prior resolved IMPORTS edges — unchanged files' resolved edges are preserved instead of churned. Force-reindex (force=true) and large-batch passes still take the existing project-wide delete + rebuild path. v0.85 measurement showed 94% of incremental-tick cost was the resolve block; this slice attacks the share attributable to IMPORTS (smallest of the three, ~7ms / 676ms on pincher-repo). CALLS (252ms) and READS (181ms) follow in subsequent PRs to keep the change small and reviewable.
